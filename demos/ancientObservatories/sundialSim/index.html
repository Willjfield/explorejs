The way we measure time is continually changing.
The time displayed here does not take into account Daylight Savings Time (DST), which has changed constantly in different jurisdictions since its invention in the early 20th century. Therefore, there is no way to accurately display the time for prior dates using DST.

You may also notice that if you enter a date before 1582, it will be off by several days. The Gregorian calendar was instituted in 1582. Any date entered prior to then is automatically converted to the Julian calendar.
The sundial presented here was found in the Villa Palombara on the Esquiline Hill in Rome.
